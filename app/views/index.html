Vision

Making Defra’s payments accessible and transparent

To provide a valuable resource for farmers, businesses, and other stakeholders who are interested in learning more about the government's agricultural support.

Benefits

We need to:
• make sure that we use public money effectively and efficiently
• provide farmers with the information they need to make informed decisions about how to apply for government support
• build trust between farmers and the government
• promote transparency and accountability in the use of public funds
• educate the public about the importance of agriculture and the role that farmers play in the UK economy
• raise awareness of the challenges facing farmers and the need for government support
• build support for government policies that support farmers

Service version history

As an agile team, we need to continually iterate our service to adapt to emerging needs after feedback from both policy teams and user research sessions. By developing the service in this way, we can help users reach the information they need as quickly as possible.

Content principles

To make sure the service works for users both inside and outside the agricultural industry, we must:

• give clear instructions on what the service includes (payment details, schemes, time period)
• only publish information that is not already published elsewhere
• use technical terms where relevant with a clear explanation of their meaning
• link to the relevant supporting guidance to give extra context where necessary
• use the GOV.UK style guide to create a tone of voice that users can trust and understand

Interaction principles

Our service must:
• meet the government service standard
• meet current web standards
• meet user and organisational needs
• be simple and intuitive to use
• work in a way that is familiar
• be usable by everyone, equally





Background

Moving from CAP regulations to GOV.UK

The UK government is working with farmers, businesses, and other stakeholders to develop a new agricultural policy framework after leaving the European Union. This new framework will support farmers to produce high-quality food, protect the environment, and create jobs in rural areas.

The ‘Find farm and land payment data’ service will show how public money is being spent to support farmers across the UK.

What data we publish

Now that we are no longer in the EU, the rules around what we must publish have changed. This means that we are no longer part of the UK Co-ordinating Body service’s Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) schemes.

Instead, under the Agriculture (Financial Assistance) Regulations 2021 we publish data for the:

• Sustainable Farming Incentive pilot
• Farming Equipment and Technology Fund

We only publish data when recipients of these schemes have received total agricultural scheme payments of £1,250 or more.

The data includes:

• full names of agreement holders and businesses
• partial postcodes, post towns and districts where the agreement holder is resident or located
• total payments received by the agreement holder in the last financial year
• a description of the activities financed by the relevant payments

Tree Health Pilot Scheme

We publish the total figure paid through the scheme as a single amount.



User Needs

As a user, I need to...

• find out who the payment was made to so that I know who is carrying out the activity on the farm.

• find out what the payment was for so that I can understand what types of activity are being conducted on the farm.

• find the service easily so that I can access the data that I need.

• have a clear explanation of what the payment descriptions mean so that I can be confident I have understood the data I am using.

• view the information published about payments made to me or my company so that I can be assured it is accurate.

• correct any errors in the data published about me so that I can exercise my statutory rights of rectification.

• search for farms and businesses with similar names so that I don't have to conduct multiple searches when I don't know the exact name of the business I'm looking for (fuzzy matching).
